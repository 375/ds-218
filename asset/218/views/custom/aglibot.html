Rodelio on dragon fruit

If you love jicama, dragon fruit is a great thing to substitute in your salads. You may think the little specs are seeds, but they’re not, they’re actually easy to eat. It’s a beautiful fruit to use as a garnish.

What’s your favorite way to cook chicken?

Poaching. You can poach with aromatics like ginger, garlic, onions, celery, carrots. Bring the water up to a full boil. Drop your chicken breasts in the water and let go for about 3-4 minutes. Turn it off and let it sit for 45 minutes.

Q & A

Which Mariano’s do you shop?

I normally shop Mariano’s Arlington Heights. My restaurant is in Mount Prospect, so it’s nearby.

What produce were you surprised to find at Mariano’s?

Mini-pineapples. I had these growing up in Hawaii.

Who is your biggest influence?

My dad. He was a navy cook. He taught me how to peel potatoes and cook chicken and burgers.

What kind of ingredients did you experiment with as a kid?

Eggs. I did eggs every which way. I just love fried eggs on rice.

How often do you change your restaurant menu?

I don’t make full changes, but I create new dishes every week.

What’s in your fridge at home right now?

Romaine lettuce, brown eggs and condiments. I’m a spice condiment freak.

What essentials should you always have on hand?

Olive oil, honey, flour and salt. Fresh peppercorn. Those are my base foundations.

Any tips on making quicker meals?

If you shop Mariano’s, a lot of the stuff is already precooked or pre-portioned. It doesn’t get any faster.

What do you love about Mariano’s?

It’s like shopping in the European markets. You get help from real artisans who know what’s going on.
